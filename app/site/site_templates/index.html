Open Up a New World

Picture it: You've just finished your work week, and it's Friday night. You contemplate throwing a rager and inviting 200 of your closest friends...
until you remember that you're not a "rager" kind of person, and that sounds like a special kind of hell dreamt up by the writers of "The Good Place". Time to curl up on the couch with a cup of tea (or a little whiskey, if you're feeling spicy) and a good book. You consult your bookshelf, only to discover that you've read everything in your house. The horror. The sadness. The total and utter devastation. The melodramatics.

We've all been there, right? (...right...?) Anyway, don't worry; we've got you. This database allows you and your friends to input reading recommendations, as well as important details such as the book's author, genre, page length, and ISBN for easy searches. So any time you find yourself at a loss for what to read, just hop onto this virtual library and check out some recommendations!

How it Works

Basically, you sign up for this mock website using what is hopefully a silly and fun fake email address. The weirder the better, here; show me what you've got.
Then, you navigate your way on over to the "Login" page, log in using aforementioned silly email, and copy your user token. The security on this website is...sketchy at best. But that's okay! This database is super exclusive; you can count on your fingers the number of people that know it exists.
Using Insomnia (or another program, maybe, you do you), write yourself some requests...
...and bam! You should be able to access the entire database, pull a single book recommendation, update the book's info, delete a book entirely (although I hope you don't), and add your own.

NOTE: I didn't Google it! These are real recommendations that are in my top 20, so I hope you find one that speaks to you.

NOTE, the sequel: Access to this database is, unfortunately, not free. Payment can be made in the form of at least one book recommendation, and can be input directly into the database.

If you're living in the South like me, there's a chance your state is trying to limit access to books that promote crazy ideas such as, "maybe cis white supremacy isn't the greatest approach to a functioning and inclusive society after all," or "women are people," or "slavery was bad".
